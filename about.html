Who I Am

Hey there! I'm Varsha Saravanan, a fourth-year at Stanford building products at the intersection of technology, people, and intention. I'm pursuing a B.S. and M.S. in Computer Science, focused on Human–Computer Interaction and Artificial Intelligence, and I spend my time turning ambiguous, high-stakes problems into thoughtful, usable systems. If it's complex, human, and worth building right, you'll probably find me there.

What Matters To Me

Attention Before Action

I pay close attention to how people experience systems, especially when their voices are easy to overlook. I try to understand context and constraints before deciding what should change.

Responsibility Over Speed

I believe innovation should move thoughtfully. I listen before acting and build with care for the consequences, not just momentum.

Small Choices Compound

I've learned that consistent, intentional decisions shape outcomes over time. I try to live that belief in the people I show up for, the products I build, and the life I'm creating.
